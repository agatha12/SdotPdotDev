In recent years SXSW has transformed into the epicenter of the music industry. For one week every year an incredible amount of the most powerful people in music come to Austin to check out acts, talk shop, and form arrangements that will shape the next year of music as most of us will know it. Brands like Doritos pay big money for an opportunity to connect with an audience that is becoming increasingly difficult to reach. Young people are immune to billboards and commercials today because they’ve grown up in a world that is saturated with content, and advertisements interrupt content. SXSW is a sure bet for big corporations who are looking to connect with young people en masse. And if you ask around, a lot of people will tell you that’s all SXSW is. For their sake, they’re probably right. But, the statement isn’t accurate. For a lot of folks like me SXSW is the one time, and the one place in the world where we can meet up with most of the people we know online. For those who are tapped into the underground subcultures defining music and art SXSW presents a vibrant network of opportunities and memorable experiences waiting to be made. I traveled from New York down to Austin for the 5th year in a row and reveled in the opportunity to see friends who I haven’t seen since last year, meet friends from the internet for the first time, make new friends, and generally enjoy the event. The artists in these photographs are people like me, from all different parts of the world. They pay their own way to get down there, and see what the universe will throw their way. Of course everyone has it in the back of their mind that they’d love to land some giant contract that will pay them a million dollars, but mostly we’re looking to have a good time, hang out with our peers, and hopefully make some fruitful connections or set some plays in motion. There is no magic save-all in music, and there aren’t any perfect events. These artists all make different styles of music, but they all have the same realistic perspective. SXSW is definitely not without its flaws, but it’s also not without its perfections. Hopefully these images, and words will help give you some insight on why I keep going back, along with a look at the backdrop that Austin presents for this incredible week of music.
