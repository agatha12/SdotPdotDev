Summer Apples NYC

New York: you can sometimes forget how quickly the vibrant cultures change from block to block. I recently made a quick trip back to NYC for work and spent the majority of my time in Chinatown, a neighborhood that shares its border with a Hasidic Jewish community. During a stroll through this eccentric lower Manhattan space, I came across an all-Asian volleyball tournament, German bmx rider Dustyn Alt, and a sketchy chop shop with a fake grocery store façade. Even the weather shifted as I shifted turfs, as new cloud cover took the day from an unbearable scorcher to a breezy cool picnic. And if you are overwhelmed by the swiftness of change, you’ll always be comforted by the things that hardly ever change. Like the majestic skyline or the familiar stench of fish markets, rotting trash, and car exhaust. But this is the infamous Big Apple, and even though gentrification is trying to rob us everyday of our precious culture, there will always be something just below the surface if you dig a little.
