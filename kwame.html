Artist Spotlight: Chef Kwame Williams

Knowing what’s in your food and where it comes from is at the fore of most people’s minds nowadays. With all the additives and processed junk out there, it surely feels like an uphill battle to stay in the know. But with chefs like Kwame Williams, owner of Vital Dining in Montclair, NJ, keeping an eye on the road for us, I feel like I can take a quick nap in the passenger seat. Shortly after winning the Food Network cooking competition “Cooks vs Cons,” Kwame left his sous chef position to begin his journey as a chef/owner. He quickly learned that success would not come easy but that inspiration and love of the culinary institute would guide him into any battle. Kwame prioritizes and pays homage to a culture of clean eating, his Jamaican heritage, and his connection to the community in all that he does. On any given day, you can find Kwame in one of the town’s locally run community gardens picking fresh heirloom tomatoes or spicy peppers straight off the vine, or delivering meal plans to the New York Giants training facility.

In my own hospitality experience, the kitchen can sometimes be an intense and wacky place. But with this humble, gentle giant on the line, the culture will start to reflect his values. And that’s why he’s winning. Below are images from our day in Montclair. Enjoy.
